Carbon Offset Fun Fact #4 - November 28, 2021


RECs can make a Statement, but Carbon Offsets make a Difference



“Additionality: (Carbon) Offsets must represent real, permanent, verified, and enforceable reductions. And most importantly, they must come from activities or project that are additional to what would occur under a business-as-usual scenario. This “additionality” requirement for offset projects is central to ensuring that the ton of emissions reductions you use as an offset is fully equivalent to a ton of emissions reductions from your operations. There is no requirement to demonstrate additionality when applying RECs to an organization’s market- based scope 2 emissions.” 1 (Please see EPA reference cited below)

So….if RECs don’t have to demonstrate additionality, does anything change in the environment when you buy RECs? Good question…….

1. Offsets and RECs: What’s the Difference, EPA Green Power Partnership, February 2018; https://www.epa.gov/sites/default/files/2018- 03/documents/gpp_guide_recs_offsets.pdf
