Carbon Offset Fun Fact #6 - December 14, 2021


Four out of Five Major Donors Prefer Carbon Offsets



Imagine you are at a cocktail party of potential major doners and one asks, “So, what are we doing about sustainability and how are you preparing our students for addressing the existential environmental threat confronting them?” Consider the following two responses:


RESPONSE #1: “We are aggressively reducing our on-campus carbon footprint and we are purchasing the environmental attributes from a 2010 North Dakota wind plant that is owned by an investment bank and is operated by the local electric utility.”

MAJOR DONOR’S FOLLOW-UP: “What’s an environmental attribute? You know what – on second thought, I’m going to refresh my drink. You’ll have to excuse me."


OR........


RESPONSE #2: “We are aggressively reducing our on-campus carbon footprint and we are supporting a carbon offset project in Rwanda that provides more efficient residential wood burning stoves that not only halve daily carbon emissions, but also reduce the time women gather wood, thereby allowing them more time with their families or finding other productive work.”

MAJOR DONOR’S FOLLOW-UP: “Wow…that sounds interesting. Please tell me more about this project. It sounds like it not only reduces our carbon footprint, but it also addresses social and environmental justice issues – something that I bet is near and dear to our students.”


Which conversation would you prefer to have?
